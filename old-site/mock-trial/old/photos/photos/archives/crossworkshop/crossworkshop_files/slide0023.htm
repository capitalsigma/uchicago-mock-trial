Your Checklist for trial
vWatch the Judge
–listen to their responses to objections,
•whether they limit a witness' answers
•how they react to certain themes presented
•how they react to different witness types
v
vWatch the Witness
–How they portray their witnesses
•often, witnesses are portrayed in similar fashions across the board
•a team’s witnesses will probably fight on cross, all offer additional information on their responses, and all invent (to some extent) 
•If one witness does it, it's likely that they all do!
–Watch for a theme
•if their witnesses are concentrating on one aspect of the case try to avoid asking questions that allow them to emphasize their theme
•Grill them on the main issues and sit down
v
vWatch the Attorney
–generally, a team is better at some things than others i.e. They may use relevance or foundation objections very well
–so be ready to defend yourself on those pieces of evidence that are only partially relevant, etc.
